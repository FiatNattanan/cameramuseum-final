About Us

Through the Lens: Camera Musuem is a dedicated project celebrating the evolution of photography. Our mission is to preserve, showcase, and share the stories behind iconic cameras that have shaped visual storytelling for generations. From the earliest box cameras to groundbreaking mid-century innovations, our collection reflects the ingenuity and artistry of photographic technology. Each piece in our gallery is not just a device; it’s a glimpse into the past, telling stories of creativity, culture, and human connection.

Ernst Herbst & Firl Travel Camera

Manufacture Period: Before 1909 Origin: Görlitz, Germany (Ernst Herbst & Firl) This classic travel camera is best known for its connection to Polish composer Mieczysław Karłowicz, who owned and used it extensively in the early 20th century. In 1909, Karłowicz tragically perished in an avalanche while skiing in Zakopane, Poland. After his death, Stanisław Barabasz developed the film left in the camera, capturing a poignant snapshot of Karłowicz’s final moments. Today, the camera and the photos it took are preserved in the Tatra Museum in Zakopane, embodying both artistic and historical significance.

Kodak Junior A

Manufacture Period: 1914-1927 Origin: Rochester, USA (Eastman Kodak) The Kodak Junior A is a folding camera that represents Kodak's innovation in early 20th-century photography. Known for its elegant design and functionality, it captured moments during an era when photography was becoming more accessible. At an unspecified time, this camera became part of Ignacy Paderewski’s memorabilia collection, adding a touch of historical and cultural significance to its story.

Argus C-3

Manufacture Period: 1939-1966 Origin: Chicago, USA (Argus Inc.) The Argus C-3, often referred to as "The Brick," is one of the most iconic cameras in photography history. Widely produced during its time, it gained global recognition for its durability and affordability. After World War II, a replica of the Argus C-3 was created and later donated to the Museum of Photography in Kraków by Jan Oberbek. This camera also made an appearance in the 2002 film Harry Potter and the Chamber of Secrets, cementing its legacy in popular culture. In 2010, the Kraków museum officially acquired the Argus C-3, where it remains a part of their collection.

Zefir Cameras

Manufacture Period: 1966 Origin: Warsaw, Poland (WZFO – Warsaw Photo-Optical Works) The Zefir camera is a fascinating example of photographic innovation that was never fully realized. Only two prototypes were created, each featuring distinct lenses: the Emitar and the Domiplan. However, due to fierce competition from manufacturers in the USSR and GDR, mass production was never achieved. In 1995, the two prototypes were gifted to the Museum of Photography in Kraków by Stefan Surde, preserving their legacy as a rare piece of photographic history.

Zenit Foto Snajper FS-12

Manufacture Period: 1988 Origin: Krasnogorsk, USSR (KMZ – Krasnogorskiy Mekhanicheskiy Zavod) The Zenit Foto Snajper FS-12 is a modernized version of the Fotosnajper, designed for long-range and precision photography. Released in 1982, it utilized the Zenit 12s camera body, offering photographers a robust and specialized tool. This camera gained popularity among professionals and hobbyists alike for its unique design and capabilities. In 2014, the Museum of Photography in Kraków acquired this remarkable camera, further enriching its extensive collection.
